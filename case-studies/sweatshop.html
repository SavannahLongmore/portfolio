Sweatshop Interactive

Immersive narrative website that turns heavy subject matter into thoughtful, respectful exploration

Framer Interaction Design Visual Design Storytelling

Overview

This project challenged our team to create an immersive website about the global impact of sweatshops. Because the topic is emotionally heavy and socially sensitive, the goal was to present difficult information in a way that was informative, respectful, and engaging — not passive or overwhelming.

My Role

UX/UI Designer

Timeline

12 weeks

Team

2 designers + 3 English students (narrative development)

Tools

Framer, Figma, Illustrator, Rive

Design Strategy

The tone needed to be somber and respectful. We chose drab colors and powerful imagery to match the gravity of the topic. The layout was structured like a narrative article, allowing users to move at their own pace while always knowing where they were in the story.

  • Context-aware navigation with a scrolling progress indicator
  • Restrained interactions to avoid trivializing the subject
  • Guided storytelling balanced with user freedom to explore

Key Interactions

Eight immersive elements were built to deepen engagement without becoming distracting.

Reflection & Growth

This was my first major project in Framer, and it pushed me to learn quickly under a tight deadline. I learned how to build immersive interactions independently, solve technical challenges through experimentation, and balance emotional sensitivity with interaction design.

Most importantly, I learned that interaction design is not about adding movement for visual interest — it's about using interaction intentionally to support meaning and deeper understanding. Several playful ideas were ultimately removed because they felt too light-hearted for the topic. Prioritizing tone and respect over novelty was a valuable lesson.
